Are you looking for adorable and practical hairstyles for your little one? Do you find yourself wondering how to keep your toddler’s hair neat and stylish at the same time?

![](/images/baby-braid-styles/baby-braid-styles-576x1024.jpg)

**Well, you’re in the right place! I’ve gathered 25 delightful baby braid styles that are perfect for toddlers.**

Whether you’re getting ready for a special occasion or just want to keep your child’s hair tidy for everyday play, these ideas will provide you with the inspiration you need.

**Let’s dive in!**

## 25 Baby Braid Styles for Toddlers in 2025 ### 1. Classic Pigtail Braids

If you’re looking for an adorable and timeless hairstyle for your toddler, classic pigtail braids are a perfect choice.

**Simply part your little one’s hair down the middle and braid each side into neat, even pigtails.**

You can tie the ends with colorful ribbons to add a fun and playful touch. This style is not only cute but also practical, keeping hair out of their face while they play.

![](/images/baby-braid-styles/3dushuhsufhusdhfehuhrweuihruer-1024x1024.png)

### 2. French Braided Crown

A French braided crown is a beautiful and elegant hairstyle for your toddler, perfect for special occasions.

**Start by parting the hair and braiding around the head, creating a crown-like effect. Adorn the braid with small, pastel-colored flowers to make it even more charming.**

This style looks like it came straight out of a fairy tale and will make your little one feel like a princess.

![](/images/baby-braid-styles/DALL%C2%B7E-2024-06-03-10.12.47-A-joyful-toddler-with-shoulder-length-hair-styled-into-a-French-braided-crown-where-the-braid-circles-the-head-like-a-crown-adorned-with-small-past.webp)

### 3. Box Braids with Beads

Box braids with beads are a stylish and fun option for toddlers with medium to long hair. Section the hair into small, even parts and braid each section, adding colorful beads at the ends.

This style not only looks great but also helps protect your child’s hair. The beads add a playful and vibrant touch, making it a favorite among kids.

![](/images/baby-braid-styles/DALL%C2%B7E-2024-06-03-10.14.18-A-toddler-with-medium-length-hair-styled-into-small-neatly-parted-box-braids-each-ending-with-colorful-beads.-The-toddler-is-wearing-a-vibrant-casu.webp)

### 4. Braided Bun

For a neat and sophisticated look, try a braided bun. Start by French braiding your toddler’s hair from the top of the head and then wrap the braid into a bun.

Secure it with a cute hairpin or elastic. This style is perfect for keeping hair tidy and is great for both everyday wear and special occasions. It’s a charming blend of elegance and practicality.

![](/images/baby-braid-styles/DALL%C2%B7E-2024-06-03-10.15.24-A-toddler-with-long-hair-styled-in-a-single-French-braid-that-wraps-into-a-neat-bun-at-the-top-of-the-head-secured-with-a-cute-hairpin.-The-toddler-i.webp)

### 5. Cornrow Braids

Cornrow braids are a fantastic option for toddlers with short to medium hair. These braids are created close to the scalp, forming neat rows that are symmetrical and stylish.

You can finish the ends with small beads for an extra pop of color. This style is not only fashionable but also helps keep your child’s hair protected and manageable.

![](/images/baby-braid-styles/DALL%C2%B7E-2024-06-03-10.16.22-A-toddler-with-short-hair-styled-into-multiple-cornrow-braids-that-are-close-to-the-scalp-with-symmetrical-cornrows-ending-with-small-beads-at-the-ti.webp)

### 6. Side Swept Braids

Side-swept braids offer a cute and casual look for your toddler. Part the hair on one side and create two French braids that sweep across the head and fall over one shoulder.

Secure the braids with colorful elastics. This style is perfect for everyday activities and keeps hair neatly in place while adding a touch of style.

![](/images/baby-braid-styles/DALL%C2%B7E-2024-06-03-10.17.24-A-toddler-with-shoulder-length-hair-styled-into-two-side-swept-French-braids-that-start-at-the-parting-and-fall-gently-over-one-shoulder-secured-with.webp)

### 7. Heart-Shaped Braids

For a unique and adorable look, try heart-shaped braids. Part your toddler’s hair and create cornrows in the shape of hearts that converge into a ponytail at the back.

This intricate design is perfect for special occasions and is sure to draw compliments. The heart shapes add a playful and loving touch to your child’s hairstyle.

![](/images/baby-braid-styles/DALL%C2%B7E-2024-06-03-10.18.15-A-toddler-with-long-hair-styled-into-heart-shaped-cornrows-that-converge-into-a-ponytail-at-the-back.-The-heart-shapes-are-detailed-and-intricate.-The.webp)

### 8. Braided Ponytail

A braided ponytail is a simple yet stylish option for toddlers with medium to long hair. Gather the hair into a high ponytail and braid it down.

Secure the braid with a bright bow at the base. This style is easy to achieve and keeps hair out of your child’s face, making it perfect for active play and everyday wear.

### 9. Waterfall Braids

Waterfall braids are a whimsical and pretty choice for your toddler. Create a waterfall braid by allowing strands to cascade down from the braid, giving a flowing effect.

This style works well with long hair and adds a touch of fairy-tale magic to your child’s look. It’s perfect for special events or whenever you want to add a bit of extra charm.

### 10. Halo Braid

A halo braid is a lovely and elegant style for your toddler, ideal for special occasions. Braid the hair around the entire head to create a halo effect, making your little one look angelic.

This style is not only beautiful but also practical, keeping hair neatly in place. It’s a perfect blend of grace and functionality, ensuring your child looks adorable all day.

### 11. Zigzag Part Braids

For a fun and quirky hairstyle, try zigzag part braids. Part your toddler’s hair in a zigzag pattern and braid each section.

This playful style adds a bit of extra flair and is sure to stand out. The zigzag parting adds a unique touch to the braids, making it a fun option for any day. It’s a simple way to make your child’s hairstyle more exciting.

### 12. Double Dutch Braids

Double Dutch braids are a great way to keep your toddler’s hair stylish and secure.

**Part the hair down the middle and create two Dutch braids that start at the front and go all the way down the back.**

This style is perfect for keeping hair out of the face and looks neat. Plus, it’s a versatile look that works for any occasion.

### 13. Fishtail Braids

Fishtail braids are a unique and beautiful option for toddlers with medium to long hair. Create two fishtail braids, one on each side, for a distinctive and textured look.

This style is perfect for adding a touch of elegance to your child’s hairstyle and is great for both casual and special occasions. It’s a bit different from regular braids and looks truly special.

### 14. Braided Space Buns

Braided space buns are a fun and playful hairstyle for toddlers. Create two high braids and wrap them into buns on top of the head.

Secure the buns with colorful elastics for an extra touch of fun. This style is perfect for active kids as it keeps the hair neatly in place while looking adorable. It’s a great choice for a playful and energetic look.

### 15. Braided Headband

A braided headband is a simple and elegant way to style your toddler’s hair. Create a braid that runs from one ear to the other, acting as a headband and holding back the rest of the hair.

This style is perfect for keeping hair out of your child’s face while adding a touch of sophistication. It’s easy to achieve and works well for any occasion.
