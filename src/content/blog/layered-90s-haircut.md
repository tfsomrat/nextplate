The 90s were a golden era for iconic hairstyles, and among the most beloved trends were the voluminous, layered haircuts that defined the decade.

From feathered ends to big, bouncy layers, the **layered 90s haircut** has stood the test of time, making a major comeback today.

![](/images/layered-90s-haircut/layered-90s-haircut-1-1024x576.jpg)

**In this article, we’ll take a trip down memory lane and explore 20 layered 90s haircut ideas that are still as stylish today as they were decades ago.**

From feathered layers with a soft flip to voluminous blowouts with center-parted bangs, these styles are perfect for anyone wanting to embrace a retro yet modern look.

## Layered 90s Haircuts

![](/images/layered-90s-haircut/layered-90s-haircut-576x1024.jpg)

### 1. Feathered Layers with Middle Part

This **classic 90s hairstyle** showcases feathered layers and a sleek middle part. The layers are **softly graduated**, adding volume and movement to the hair while maintaining a natural flow.

To achieve this look, ask your stylist to create **long layers that frame the face** and feather the ends for that signature 90s texture.

Use a round brush when blow-drying to emphasize the feathered effect. This style suits straight or slightly wavy hair, providing a subtle, elegant look reminiscent of 90s icons.

![](/images/layered-90s-haircut/image-654.jpeg)

### 2. Full Voluminous Blowout Layers

A **blowout with full layers** gives off strong 90s supermodel vibes, featuring bouncy, voluminous hair. The layers begin at the chin, flowing into fuller ends, creating a cascading effect.

Use a large-barrel curling iron or a round brush while blow-drying to enhance volume and lift at the roots. The key to this look is **volume**, so volumizing mousse or spray is essential.

This style works best with medium to thick hair and can be worn for both casual and formal events.

![](/images/layered-90s-haircut/image-655-819x1024.jpeg)

@[telleish\_hair\_studio](https://www.instagram.com/telleish_hair_studio/)

### 3. Layered Curtain Bangs with Wispy Ends

Curtain bangs were a **90s staple**, and when combined with layered hair, they create a soft and chic look.

The bangs are parted in the middle, framing the face beautifully, while the wispy ends add texture and lightness. To style, blow-dry the bangs with a round brush to keep them soft and flick them outward slightly.

The layers on the rest of the hair start at chin length, gradually getting longer, creating movement. This look is perfect for anyone wanting a low-maintenance yet trendy 90s style.

![](/images/layered-90s-haircut/image-656.jpeg)

@[salonemagedayspa](https://www.instagram.com/salonemagedayspa/)

### 4. Tousled Layered Bob with Side Bangs

For a more casual and laid-back look, the **tousled layered bob with side bangs** is ideal. The layers add dimension and volume to the bob, while the side bangs frame the face, giving it a relaxed yet styled appearance.

To achieve the tousled effect, use a texturizing spray or mousse and scrunch the hair while blow-drying.

This cut is great for those with medium-length hair looking for an easy, everyday 90s-inspired hairstyle. The layers make it versatile, allowing for both sleek and messy styles.

![](/images/layered-90s-haircut/image-657.jpeg)

@[jamesplainhair](https://www.instagram.com/jamesplainhair/)

### 5. Classic Long Layers with Curtain Bangs

This **classic long layered haircut with curtain bangs** channels strong 90s energy. The layers are strategically cut to add movement and lightness, especially around the face.

The **curtain bangs** frame the face softly, blending effortlessly into the longer layers. To achieve this, ask your stylist for face-framing layers that start around the cheekbones and taper down, with wispy, center-parted bangs.

Blow-dry the bangs with a round brush for a voluminous, sweeping effect. This style is perfect for those with longer hair who want a chic, **low-maintenance look** that retains its volume and flow.

### 6. Sleek Layered Blowout

A **sleek layered blowout** combines sharp layers with a smooth, polished finish. This 90s-inspired style emphasizes smooth layers that curve under at the ends, adding bounce and structure.

For this look, use a round brush while blow-drying the layers inward to create a **sculpted effect**. A hair straightener can also help refine the sleekness.

This hairstyle is ideal for medium to thick hair types and provides a polished, put-together look that works well for both day and night.

### 7. Subtle Layers with Middle Part

This haircut features **subtle layers and a middle part**, evoking a minimalist 90s vibe. The layers are barely noticeable but add just enough texture and movement to prevent the hair from looking flat.

To maintain the sleekness of the style, use a straightener to keep the hair smooth and a little serum for shine. The middle part adds a timeless, **balanced symmetry** to the face.

This cut is great for those who prefer a more understated, everyday 90s-inspired hairstyle with just a hint of layers.

### 8. Full-bodied Layers with Side-swept Bangs

This **full-bodied layered haircut** features side-swept bangs that add a flattering frame to the face. The 90s influence is clear in the voluminous layers, which are styled to lift away from the face, creating a **soft, bouncy look**.

The side-swept bangs add a youthful, playful touch, blending seamlessly with the overall hairstyle. This look is perfect for those seeking a **glamorous, retro 90s vibe** with a modern twist.

This look is perfect for adding volume to fine hair and for anyone who wants a casual yet chic 90s vibe.
