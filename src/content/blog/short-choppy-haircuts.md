**Short choppy haircuts** are the perfect fusion of boldness and texture, offering a carefree yet stylish look that’s both modern and versatile.

These cuts are all about creating movement with edgy, choppy layers while keeping the length short for easy maintenance.

![](/images/short-choppy-haircuts/short-choppy-haircuts-1-1024x576.jpg)

**In this article, we’ve rounded up 23 Short Choppy Haircut Ideas to inspire your next salon visit. From daring pixie cuts to playful bobs, each style offers something different, ensuring there’s a cut for every hair type and personality.**

Whether you’re looking for a structured, voluminous pixie or a fun and vibrant choppy bob, these hairstyles are guaranteed to give you a fresh, modern look with minimal effort.

## Short Choppy Haircuts

![](/images/short-choppy-haircuts/short-choppy-haircuts-576x1024.jpg)

### 1. Edgy Undercut Pixie

This **undercut pixie** offers an edgy and modern twist to short hair. The top layers are left long and choppy, allowing for textured movement, while the sides are shaved close to the scalp for a bold contrast.

This cut is perfect for anyone wanting a mix of femininity and edginess. To achieve this look, ask for **choppy layers** on top and a tapered undercut on the sides.

Styling is simple: apply a lightweight mousse or wax to the top layers for added definition and volume, allowing the natural texture to show.

### 2. Shaggy Mullet with Textured Layers

The **shaggy mullet** embraces retro vibes with a modern flair. This cut features shorter layers around the crown, while the back is left slightly longer, giving a cool, undone look.

The choppy texture adds volume and movement, making it ideal for those with fine hair. Achieving this hairstyle involves asking your stylist for shorter, **textured layers** at the top and leaving more length at the back.

Add a bit of mousse to enhance the natural waves or use a texturizing spray to create a tousled effect. It’s perfect for anyone wanting a carefree yet stylish haircut.

![](/images/short-choppy-haircuts/image-570-1024x1024.jpeg)

### 3. Choppy Boyish Pixie Cut

This **boyish pixie cut** is defined by its short, close-cropped back and sides, with a longer, choppy top.

The shortness around the nape and ears contrasts sharply with the textured, voluminous top, giving a youthful and playful look.

To get this style, ask for a **tapered cut** at the back, with the top section left longer for styling versatility. Use a light pomade or wax to define the top layers and create piecey texture.

### 4. Short Choppy Bob with Layers

This **choppy bob** offers both softness and structure, making it ideal for anyone looking for a stylish yet manageable cut. The layers are cut to add volume around the crown and texture to the ends, giving a fuller appearance to fine hair.

The bob length is perfect for framing the face while keeping it lightweight. To achieve this, ask your stylist for a **layered bob** with choppy ends.

For styling, use a round brush while blow-drying to give volume to the roots and a slight wave to the layers. It’s a versatile cut that can be dressed up or down.

### 5. Choppy Bob with Face-Framing Layers

This **choppy bob with face-framing layers** gives a fresh and youthful appearance, perfect for those who want a short cut with dimension. The face-framing layers soften the overall look, while the choppy texture adds volume.

To achieve this style, ask for a **chin-length bob** with textured, choppy layers around the face. Blow-dry with a round brush to enhance the layers or use a flat iron for a sleeker finish.

Apply a light styling cream to bring out the layers and keep the look smooth but textured. This cut is ideal for adding dimension to finer hair types.

### 6. Voluminous Feathered Lob

This **voluminous feathered lob** is all about movement and body. The choppy layers throughout the hair create a feathered look that adds texture and volume to the lob. The layers are concentrated around the crown to give extra height and fullness.

To achieve this, ask for a **layered lob** with feathered ends. Blow-dry the hair using a round brush, focusing on lifting the roots for volume.

Add a bit of hair mousse or texturizing spray to bring out the feathered layers. This look is perfect for anyone wanting a soft, airy feel to their short haircut.
