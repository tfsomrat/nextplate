## What is Feathered Hair?

Feathered hair is a style characterized by its light, airy layers that give the hair a soft and voluminous appearance.

**The term “feathered” refers to the way the hair is cut and styled to create a look similar to feathers, with each layer tapering to a fine point, resembling the delicate, layered structure of a bird’s feathers.**

![](/images/feathered-hair/Feathered-Hair-ideas-for-2025-683x1024.webp)

This technique involves cutting the hair at an angle, typically using a razor or shears, to create wispy, textured ends that blend seamlessly with the rest of the hair.

**The feathered hairstyle gained popularity in the 1970s, thanks to celebrities like Farrah Fawcett, whose iconic feathered hair became a defining look of the decade.**

The style can be adapted to various hair lengths and types, making it versatile and suitable for different face shapes and personal preferences.

1.  **Layers**: Feathered haircuts often involve multiple layers, with the shortest layers typically starting around the crown of the head and gradually lengthening towards the ends. These layers add volume and movement to the hair.
2.  **Textured Ends**: The ends of the hair are finely textured, creating a soft and wispy effect. This is achieved through cutting techniques that thin out the hair at the ends, reducing bulk and creating a feather-like appearance.
3.  **Volume and Movement**: The layered and textured nature of feathered haircuts adds natural volume and movement, making the hair look fuller and more dynamic.
4.  **Versatility**: Feathered hair can be styled in various ways, including straight, wavy, or curly. It can be worn with or without bangs, and the layers can be adjusted to suit different hair lengths and textures.
