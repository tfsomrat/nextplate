The 2000s was an iconic era for fashion, music, and, of course, **hairstyles** especially for Black women.

From bold, textured looks to sleek, polished styles, the decade offered a wide range of options that celebrated versatility and creativity.

![](/images/2000s-hairstyles-black-women/2000s-Hairstyles-Black-Women-1-1-1024x576.jpg)

The beauty of 2000s hairstyles was their ability to mix cultural heritage with fresh, modern twists, making them unforgettable even today.

In this article, we explore **26 of the most iconic 2000s hairstyles for Black women**.

Whether you’re feeling nostalgic or looking to bring back some of these timeless looks, each hairstyle offers something unique from protective styles like **micro braids and faux locs** to edgy favorites like **the braided mohawk** and **asymmetrical bob**.

## 2000s Hairstyles Black Women

![](/images/2000s-hairstyles-black-women/2000s-Hairstyles-Black-Women-32-576x1024.jpg)

### 1. Micro Braids

Micro braids are **tiny individual braids** created using small sections of hair. They’re versatile and can be styled in various ways, such as in ponytails, buns, or even left loose.

This style offers a sleek look with **long-lasting wear**, often lasting weeks with proper care. Due to the size, micro braids take time to install but provide a **low-maintenance** option once done.

Be sure to moisturize the scalp regularly and avoid pulling too tightly on the edges to protect the hairline from damage. Perfect for women looking for a **classic 2000s braided look** that can be styled up or down.

![](/images/2000s-hairstyles-black-women/2000s-hairstyles-black-women-683x1024.jpg)

### 2. Crimped Hair

Crimped hair was a huge trend in the 2000s, adding **texture and volume** to straight or wavy hair. This look is achieved using a crimping iron, which creates zigzag waves throughout the hair.

Crimped hair can be worn **loose or styled into updos**, giving it a playful, voluminous effect. The crimped texture works well on both relaxed and natural hair, providing a fuller appearance.

This hairstyle is great for adding dimension to your look while keeping the **retro 2000s vibe** alive. Pair it with accessories like headbands or barrettes for an extra touch of style.

![](/images/2000s-hairstyles-black-women/2000s-hairstyles-black-women-1-683x1024.jpg)

### 3. Relaxed Hair

Relaxed hair involves using chemical treatments to **straighten natural hair**, resulting in a smooth, sleek finish. This style was very popular in the 2000s, providing a **polished and versatile look**.

Relaxed hair can be worn in various styles, from sleek ponytails to straight-down styles. To maintain healthy relaxed hair, regular moisturizing and deep conditioning are essential to prevent breakage.

A **flat iron** can be used to achieve additional smoothness, but it’s important to avoid over-processing to keep the hair in good condition. Relaxed hair offers an **effortlessly chic look** for both casual and formal occasions.

![](/images/2000s-hairstyles-black-women/2000s-hairstyles-black-women-2-683x1024.jpg)

### 4. Asymmetrical Bob

The asymmetrical bob features **one side longer than the other**, giving it an edgy, stylish look. This cut became iconic in the early 2000s, often worn by celebrities for its bold structure.

The key to achieving this style is having a sharp cut that accentuates the asymmetry. It’s a low-maintenance style but still offers a **modern, sophisticated edge**. You can wear it straight or with soft curls for added volume.

The asymmetrical bob is a perfect choice for those wanting to make a statement with their hair while keeping it **easy to manage and style**.

![](/images/2000s-hairstyles-black-women/2000s-hairstyles-black-women-26-683x1024.jpg)

### 5. Finger Waves

Finger waves are a classic style that made a resurgence in the 2000s, known for their **sleek, sculpted waves** that lie close to the head.

This style is achieved by molding the hair into S-shaped waves using a comb and setting lotion. Finger waves are perfect for **shorter hair** or even cropped cuts, offering a retro yet elegant look.

This style is best for those who want a sophisticated, smooth appearance. It works well for special occasions but can also be an everyday style with the right maintenance, offering a **timeless and chic finish**.

![](/images/2000s-hairstyles-black-women/2000s-hairstyles-black-women-27-683x1024.jpg)

### 6. Box Braids with Beads

Box braids are thick, individual braids, often extended for added length. In the 2000s, these braids were frequently adorned with **beads at the ends**, adding a touch of personality and style.

The beads can be of various sizes and colors, allowing for **creative expression**. Box braids are a **protective style**, keeping natural hair tucked away, making them low-maintenance and long-lasting.

The added beads give the style a playful, traditional look. To maintain your box braids, keep the scalp moisturized and wrap the hair at night to preserve the look for weeks.

![](/images/2000s-hairstyles-black-women/2000s-hairstyles-black-women-5-683x1024.jpg)

### 7. High Ponytail with Swoop Bang

The high ponytail with a **swooped bang** was a defining hairstyle of the 2000s. The ponytail is placed high on the head for a sleek and polished look, while the bang is styled to swoop dramatically across the forehead.

This hairstyle works well for **straight or relaxed hair**, adding height and a touch of glamour. You can add extensions to create a fuller ponytail or keep it natural for a more understated style.

The swoop bang adds a **flattering frame to the face**, making this look perfect for casual and formal events alike.

![](/images/2000s-hairstyles-black-women/2000s-hairstyles-black-women-28-683x1024.jpg)

### 8. Twist-Out

A twist-out involves twisting the hair into **two-strand twists** and then unraveling them to reveal **defined curls**. This is a great way to style natural hair, providing volume and a bouncy finish.

The key to a successful twist-out is using a good styling cream or gel to ensure that the curls hold their shape. Once the twists are taken out, the hair should be gently fluffed for extra volume.

Twist-outs offer a **versatile, low-heat style** that can last for days with proper maintenance, making them perfect for natural hair wearers seeking a textured, curly look.

![](/images/2000s-hairstyles-black-women/2000s-hairstyles-black-women-7-683x1024.jpg)

### 9. Straight Weave with Part

The straight weave with a **middle or side part** offers a smooth, polished appearance that was highly popular in the 2000s. The weave adds length and fullness to the hair, creating a sleek, sophisticated look.

This style is easy to manage and perfect for **everyday wear** or special occasions. You can customize the part according to your preference either in the middle for a classic look or on the side for more volume.

To maintain, avoid over-styling with heat and keep the weave moisturized to prevent dryness and tangling.

![](/images/2000s-hairstyles-black-women/2000s-hairstyles-black-women-8-683x1024.jpg)

### 10. Pixie Cut

The pixie cut is a **short, cropped style** that’s bold and easy to maintain. Popular in the 2000s, this look highlights facial features, especially the eyes and cheekbones.

The pixie cut can be styled sleek and smooth or tousled for a more casual vibe. It’s an excellent choice for women who prefer **low-maintenance hairstyles** that still make a strong statement.

Regular trims are necessary to keep the style sharp, but the simplicity of the pixie cut makes it ideal for those looking for an effortless yet stylish haircut.

![](/images/2000s-hairstyles-black-women/2000s-hairstyles-black-women-29-683x1024.jpg)

### 11. Cornrows with Extensions

Cornrows are **tight, close-to-the-scalp braids**, often enhanced with extensions for added length. This style offers a neat and structured look while protecting natural hair.

In the 2000s, cornrows with extensions were worn in various patterns, from straight-back rows to intricate designs. Adding extensions provides versatility, allowing for longer lengths and more creative styling options.

Cornrows are a **protective hairstyle**, minimizing the need for daily manipulation. To maintain, regularly moisturize the scalp and avoid leaving the braids in for too long to prevent hair damage.

![](/images/2000s-hairstyles-black-women/2000s-hairstyles-black-women-10-683x1024.jpg)

### 12. Half-Up Half-Down

The half-up half-down style is a versatile look that involves pulling the top half of the hair into a bun or ponytail, while the rest is left down.

This hairstyle works well for **braided, relaxed, or natural hair** and offers a balance of formal and casual vibes. In the 2000s, this look was often paired with accessories like hair clips or bands.

It’s easy to achieve and can be modified with waves or curls for added texture. The half-up half-down style provides a **simple yet elegant look** that’s suitable for any occasion.

![](/images/2000s-hairstyles-black-women/2000s-hairstyles-black-women-31-683x1024.jpg)

### 13. Side-Swept Curls

Side-swept curls involve parting the hair deeply to one side and allowing the **curls to cascade over the shoulder**.

This glamorous style was popular in the 2000s, adding a touch of elegance to natural or relaxed hair. Defined curls can be achieved using a curling iron or flexi rods, and the deep side part creates volume.

This style is perfect for special occasions, offering a romantic, soft look. To maintain the curls, use a lightweight gel or cream and refresh the style with a diffuser or water mist.

![](/images/2000s-hairstyles-black-women/2000s-hairstyles-black-women-12-683x1024.jpg)

### 14. Afro Puff

An Afro puff is a simple yet striking style where natural hair is **gathered into a high or low puff**. This style is perfect for women with **kinky or coily hair**, offering a bold, defined shape.

The Afro puff became a symbol of pride in natural hair during the 2000s, and it remains a favorite for those embracing their natural texture.

To achieve the look, hair is usually combed up and secured with a hairband, leaving the puff full and voluminous. It’s a **low-maintenance style** that still makes a statement.

![](/images/2000s-hairstyles-black-women/2000s-hairstyles-black-women-13-683x1024.jpg)

### 15. Bantu Knots

Bantu knots are **small coiled sections of hair** twisted into tight buns across the scalp. This style was a hit in the 2000s, offering both a protective style and a bold, edgy look.

Bantu knots can be worn alone for a standout appearance or unraveled to create **voluminous curls**. The style works well for natural hair and can be enhanced with accessories like hair jewels.

Bantu knots are also a great **heat-free option**, protecting the hair from damage while offering a stylish, cultural expression.

![](/images/2000s-hairstyles-black-women/2000s-hairstyles-black-women-25-683x1024.jpg)

### 16. Two-Strand Twists

Two-strand twists are created by twisting two sections of hair together, providing a **protective style** that’s great for natural hair. This style was popular in the 2000s for its versatility and ease.

It can be worn loose or pinned into updos. The twists can be thick or thin depending on the desired look, and they often last for several days with proper care.

Two-strand twists help to **define curls** once unraveled and are perfect for women looking for a **heat-free, low-manipulation** hairstyle.

![](/images/2000s-hairstyles-black-women/2000s-hairstyles-black-women-14-683x1024.jpg)

### 17. Sleek Low Bun

A sleek low bun is a **smooth, polished hairstyle** that sits at the nape of the neck. Achieved by pulling the hair back tightly and securing it into a bun, this look was a go-to for formal events in the 2000s.

It works well for **straight or relaxed hair** and is ideal for creating a sophisticated, neat appearance.

Hair gel or pomade can be used to ensure a sleek finish, and the style can be adorned with accessories like barrettes or hairpins for extra flair. It’s perfect for a **minimalist yet elegant look**.

![](/images/2000s-hairstyles-black-women/2000s-hairstyles-black-women-30-683x1024.jpg)

### 18. Curly Fro

The curly fro is a voluminous, **natural hair style** that embraces big, bold curls. Popular in the early 2000s, this hairstyle celebrates natural texture and requires minimal styling products to enhance the curls.

The curly fro works best with moisturized hair, and it can be shaped using a pick or fingers to create a rounded, fluffy look.

This style offers a carefree, **retro-inspired vibe** that’s perfect for those who love to show off their natural curls. It’s a bold statement hairstyle that stands out while being easy to maintain.

![](/images/2000s-hairstyles-black-women/2000s-hairstyles-black-women-16-683x1024.jpg)

### 19. Braided Mohawk

The braided mohawk combines **cornrows on the sides** with a high section of braids or loose curls in the middle. This edgy style was popular in the 2000s for its daring and bold look.

The sides are braided tightly to the scalp, while the center is left voluminous for contrast. It’s a statement hairstyle that offers a unique, **rock-chic aesthetic**.

The braided mohawk works well with both natural and relaxed hair and can be customized with different braid patterns or added extensions.

![](/images/2000s-hairstyles-black-women/2000s-hairstyles-black-women-17-683x1024.jpg)

### 20. Faux Locs

Faux locs are **temporary dreadlocks** created using extensions, allowing women to experiment with the locs look without commitment. This style gained popularity in the 2000s as a protective and versatile option.

Faux locs can be styled in various lengths and colors, offering a natural, textured appearance. They are low-maintenance and can last for weeks, making them ideal for those looking to protect their natural hair while enjoying the look of locs.

The **natural, bohemian vibe** of faux locs made them a hit in the 2000s.

![](/images/2000s-hairstyles-black-women/2000s-hairstyles-black-women-18-683x1024.jpg)

### 21. Side Part with Volume

The side part with volume is a sleek, voluminous style achieved by creating a **deep side part** and adding height to one side of the hair.

This look was a favorite in the 2000s for its **glamorous appeal**. The side part can be worn with relaxed or natural hair, often enhanced with curls or waves for extra texture.

To create volume, hair is teased or styled with rollers, and a light hairspray can be used to hold the look in place. This hairstyle adds drama and sophistication to any outfit.

![](/images/2000s-hairstyles-black-women/2000s-hairstyles-black-women-19-683x1024.jpg)

### 22. Beehive Updo

The beehive updo is a high, structured hairstyle that **towers above the head**, reminiscent of the 1960s but revived in the 2000s.

This retro-inspired look involves teasing the hair at the crown to create height and securing it in place with pins and hairspray. The beehive works well for **both natural and relaxed hair**, offering a bold, statement look.

It’s perfect for formal events or occasions where a dramatic hairstyle is needed. The beehive updo is a **timeless, vintage-inspired style** with a modern twist.

![](/images/2000s-hairstyles-black-women/2000s-hairstyles-black-women-20-683x1024.jpg)

### 23. Gelled Edges

Gelled edges involve **styling the baby hairs** along the hairline into smooth, defined patterns using gel. This technique became iconic in the 2000s, adding a sleek, polished finish to any hairstyle.

Gelled edges can be paired with braids, buns, or ponytails for a clean, refined look. This style works for both natural and relaxed hair, and the patterns can range from simple swoops to intricate designs.

Keeping the edges moisturized and using a soft brush helps to maintain the look throughout the day.

![](/images/2000s-hairstyles-black-women/2000s-hairstyles-black-women-21-683x1024.jpg)

### 24. Colorful Streaks

Adding **colorful streaks** to natural or relaxed hair became a huge trend in the 2000s. Bright colors like red, blue, or purple were added as highlights or lowlights, giving the hair a fun, vibrant look.

This style works well with braids, weaves, or loose hair, offering a pop of color that stands out. The streaks can be temporary or permanent, depending on the desired effect.

Colorful streaks provide a bold and playful way to experiment with hair without committing to a full-color transformation.

![](/images/2000s-hairstyles-black-women/2000s-hairstyles-black-women-22-683x1024.jpg)

### 25. Crochet Braids

Crochet braids are created by **crocheting extensions into cornrowed hair**, offering a natural and voluminous look. Popular in the 2000s, this style allows for various curl patterns and lengths, making it versatile for different hair textures.

Crochet braids are easy to install and remove, making them a low-maintenance option for those who want the look of braids without the time commitment.

The **soft, curly appearance** of crochet braids gives the hair a full, bouncy finish that lasts for weeks.

![](/images/2000s-hairstyles-black-women/2000s-hairstyles-black-women-23-683x1024.jpg)

### 26. Shaggy Layers

Shaggy layers were a popular cut in the 2000s, adding **texture and movement** to relaxed or straightened hair.

This style involves cutting the hair into uneven, choppy layers that give it a tousled, carefree look. Shaggy layers work well with shoulder-length to long hair and can be styled with a blow dryer or curling iron for extra volume.

This hairstyle is great for women who want a **low-maintenance cut** that still offers plenty of body and texture, perfect for casual and everyday wear.

![](/images/2000s-hairstyles-black-women/2000s-hairstyles-black-women-24-683x1024.jpg)
