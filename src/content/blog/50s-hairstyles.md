The 1950s was a decade of glamour, elegance, and unforgettable style, where hairstyles became iconic representations of beauty and personality.

From voluminous bouffants to sleek Hollywood waves, the hairstyles of the 50s were a fusion of sophistication and charm, often inspired by screen legends like Marilyn Monroe, Audrey Hepburn, and Elizabeth Taylor.

![](/images/50s-hairstyles/50s-hairstyles-1-1024x576.jpg)

**In this article, 22 50s Hairstyles Ideas, we’re diving into some of the most beloved hairstyles of the decade, each with step-by-step insights to help you recreate these vintage looks.**

Whether you’re looking for a soft, romantic bob or a bold bouffant, this list has everything you need to add a touch of 50s magic to your hair routine.

## 50s Hairstyles

![](/images/50s-hairstyles/50s-hairstyles-576x1024.jpg)

### 1. Voluminous Hollywood Waves

This **glamorous Hollywood-inspired hairstyle** was a staple in the 1950s, adding a touch of drama and elegance.

The voluminous curls are created by sectioning the hair and using a **curling iron** or rollers to achieve tight curls, which are then brushed out gently for a soft, cohesive wave.

To achieve this look, apply **volumizing mousse** and set the curls with a strong-hold hairspray. This hairstyle beautifully frames the face, adding an old-Hollywood charm.

![](/images/50s-hairstyles/image-861-840x1024.jpeg)

@[missbeetownsend](https://www.instagram.com/missbeetownsend/)

### 2. Retro Side-Swept Curls

The side-swept curls are a perfect representation of the 1950s era, exuding elegance and sophistication.

This look is achieved by parting the hair deeply to one side and curling medium-sized sections towards the face.

The curls are then pinned and allowed to set for a polished finish. Brush out the curls gently to form soft waves, and tuck one side behind the ear to highlight the **side-swept look**.

This style works well with both casual and formal attire, adding a touch of vintage flair that feels timeless.

![](/images/50s-hairstyles/image-862-1024x1024.jpeg)

@[katelyn.rose.x](https://www.instagram.com/katelyn.rose.x/)

### 3. Classic Hollywood Waves

The Hollywood waves are timeless and perfect for achieving a glamorous 50s look. Use a large-barrel curling iron to create big, **structured waves**, brushing through lightly to keep the waves soft and smooth.

This style is ideal for evening events and complements vintage dresses. It’s a versatile look that can work on various hair lengths, bringing sophistication and elegance to any attire.

![](/images/50s-hairstyles/image-882.jpeg)

### 4. Pink Pin-Up Roll with Waves

This hairstyle brings a fun twist to 50s styles with its **bright pink hue** and pin-up roll. Start by sectioning a small portion of hair at the front, rolling it into a voluminous pin-up roll, and securing it with bobby pins.

Style the rest of the hair in loose waves for added texture. This playful look combines the retro charm of the 50s with a modern color, making it perfect for themed parties or a bold fashion statement.

![](/images/50s-hairstyles/image-883.jpeg)

### 5. Marilyn Monroe’s Classic Curls

Inspired by Marilyn Monroe, this hairstyle embodies the 50s with short, bouncy curls that frame the face.

Use a **curling iron** or hot rollers to curl small sections, then gently brush through the curls to create soft, full waves.

A side part enhances the vintage look, adding volume and shape around the crown. This style is best suited for shorter hair and provides a playful yet glamorous look.

Pair it with **bold lipstick** for a true 1950s feel, perfect for recreating Monroe’s signature style.

![](/images/50s-hairstyles/image-864.jpeg)

@[admiringmarilyn](https://www.instagram.com/admiringmarilyn/)

### 6. Glamorous Long Waves with Hat

Long, soft waves were popular in the 50s, especially when paired with accessories like **wide-brim hats**.

This look involves curling long hair with a large barrel curling iron, then gently brushing the curls for soft waves. Finish with hairspray to keep the waves in place.

Adding a vintage hat enhances the classic, feminine appeal of the hairstyle. This style is ideal for outdoor or formal events, as it combines elegance with a subtle retro charm.

![](/images/50s-hairstyles/image-866.jpeg)

### 7. Retro Scarf Updo

The scarf updo is a functional yet stylish 50s look, perfect for a vintage aesthetic. Start by pulling the hair into a loose bun or roll at the crown, securing it with bobby pins.

Place a **vibrant scarf** around the head, tying it at the top or side for an authentic touch.

This style suits both casual outings and vintage-themed events, and it’s easy to achieve at home. The scarf adds color and flair while keeping the hair in place.

![](/images/50s-hairstyles/image-867.jpeg)

### 8. Sleek Brigitte Bardot-Inspired Half-Updo

Inspired by Brigitte Bardot, this sleek half-updo combines elegance and simplicity. Begin by parting the hair in the center and pulling the top section back.

Tease lightly at the crown to add subtle volume, then secure it with a bobby pin. Leave some soft strands around the face for a romantic, **effortless look**.

This hairstyle highlights facial features and suits both casual and formal outfits, making it a timeless 50s-inspired style.

![](/images/50s-hairstyles/image-868-819x1024.jpeg)

### 9. Voluminous Blonde Beehive

The **blonde beehive** hairstyle epitomizes 50s glamour with height and volume. To achieve this look, start by backcombing the crown heavily, then smooth the top layer and secure it with bobby pins.

Leave the remaining hair in loose waves to add softness. A few black bobby pins on the side give it a vintage touch.

This hairstyle is perfect for making a bold statement at formal events, pairing well with winged eyeliner and nude lips for that classic, sophisticated feel.

### 10. Classic Pinned Waves

This elegant, pinned wave hairstyle brings a polished, vintage feel. Begin by creating deep waves with a curling iron, then use **bobby pins** to secure the sides tightly near the scalp, leaving the rest of the hair to flow naturally.

This style works well for both medium and long hair, especially when complemented by a pearl necklace or classic accessories. Perfect for a formal event, this look embodies the timeless charm of the 50s.

This playful yet chic hairstyle combines vintage curls with a bow for a pin-up inspired look. Start by curling the hair with a medium-sized iron, then brush gently to form soft, structured waves.

Place a **bright bow** at the top for a retro feel. This style is ideal for adding personality to your outfit, especially for themed parties or retro photoshoots. Paired with red lips, it captures the 50s essence beautifully.

Inspired by Audrey Hepburn, this style features **short, blunt bangs** with soft, shoulder-length waves.

The bangs are cut just above the eyebrows, giving a youthful, chic look, while the rest of the hair is curled softly to frame the face.

This hairstyle suits those looking to make a stylish yet subtle statement and works best with sleek, polished makeup. It’s perfect for casual outings or adding an iconic, feminine touch to any outfit.

### 13. Vintage Pin Curls with Scarf

Pin curls were a popular 50s style, often paired with scarves for a chic look. To create this hairstyle, section the hair into small pieces, curl them tightly, and pin each curl flat against the scalp. Allow them to set and release for defined curls.

Add a **colorful scarf** tied around the crown to complete the vintage look. This style offers both elegance and practicality, keeping the curls in place while adding a fashionable accessory.

This curled bob style, often worn by redheads in the 50s, exudes sophistication. The look is achieved by curling short hair with a small curling iron to create soft waves, then brushing them gently for a smooth finish.

The curls frame the face and highlight facial features, making this style ideal for short-haired individuals. The **vibrant red color** further enhances the vintage appeal, making it a timeless look for formal events.
