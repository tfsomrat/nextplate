## What is Trad Goth Makeup?

Trad Goth makeup is a **distinctive style that originated in the late 1970s and early 1980s,** closely associated with the **gothic subculture and its music scene.**

![](/images/trad-goth-eye-makeup/trad-goth-eye-makeup-576x1024.jpg)

**“Trad” stands for “traditional,”** reflecting the classic elements that define this makeup style.

**Here’s what typically characterizes Trad Goth makeup:**

### Key Features of Trad Goth Makeup:

1.  **Dramatic Eyes**: The eyes are the focal point in Trad Goth’s makeup. This often includes heavy, dark eyeliner, bold eyeshadow in black or deep hues, and exaggerated winged liner. Smokey eyes and intricate designs, like spider webs or bat wings, are also common.
2.  **Pale Complexion**: A very pale or even white base makeup is used to create a stark contrast with the dark eye and lip makeup. This look often includes matte foundation and powder.
3.  **Dark Lips**: Black or deep red lipstick is a staple in Trad Goth makeup. The lips are usually sharply defined and matte, adding to the overall dramatic effect.
4.  **Eyebrows**: Eyebrows are typically darkened and often sharply defined. In some variations, they might be reshaped or extended to create a more dramatic look.
5.  **Accents and Details**: Trad Goth makeup might also include additional details such as gothic symbols, lace patterns, or small designs drawn around the eyes or on the face using eyeliner.

### Inspirations and Influences

Trad **Goth makeup draws heavy inspiration from gothic rock and post-punk bands** like Siouxsie and the Banshees, Bauhaus, and The Cure.

**Siouxsie Sioux, in particular, is a significant influence, known for her dramatic eye makeup and bold fashion choices.**

_The style also incorporates elements from Victorian and Edwardian fashion, as well as from horror and gothic literature._

### Achieving the Trad Goth Look

To achieve a Trad Goth makeup look, you’ll need a few key products:

*   Matte foundation in a shade lighter than your natural skin tone.
*   Black eyeliner (liquid or pencil) for creating bold lines and intricate designs.
*   Dark eyeshadow palettes with shades of black, grey, and deep colors like burgundy or purple.
*   Black or dark red lipstick, preferably matte.
*   Mascara and false lashes enhance the dramatic eye effect.

**Trad Goth makeup is about embracing darkness, mystery, and a touch of theatricality.**

## 15 Trade Goth Eye Makeup Ideas ### 1. Classic Smokey Eye with Winged Liner

If you’re looking for that timeless goth look, the classic smokey eye with winged liner is a must-try. Start by blending dark black eyeshadow into grey on your lids.

_The secret to nailing this look is in the blending—soft and seamless. Finish with a sharp, extended winged eyeliner for that dramatic flair._

**Don’t forget to heavily coat your lashes with mascara to add volume and intensity.**

Bold black eyebrows will frame your eyes perfectly, completing this iconic gothic look. It’s bold, dramatic, and perfect for any gothic occasion.

![](/images/trad-goth-eye-makeup/ksdajfhskfjhsdakjfdsaf-771x1024.png)

### 2. Gothic Cut Crease

Ever tried a cut crease? It’s a game-changer, especially with a gothic twist. Begin with deep black eyeshadow on your lids.

_Use a white eyeshadow or eyeliner to create a sharp cut crease that contrasts beautifully with the black. Define the crease with black eyeliner to make it pop._

**Smudge some dark shadow along your lower lash line for that extra drama.**

This look is all about precision and contrast, making your eyes the center of attention. It’s a striking look that’s sure to turn heads.

![](/images/trad-goth-eye-makeup/sdiofpjeusfihdfhkjsdfdsfd-771x1024.png)

### 3. Spider Web Liner

Want to add some spooky flair to your makeup? Try the spider web liner. Start with black eyeshadow on your lids.

_Then, using black eyeliner, draw a spider web design extending from the outer corner of your eye._

**Keep the rest of your eye makeup dark and mysterious to let the web stand out.**

This design is perfect for Halloween or any time you want to add a little extra creepiness to your look. It’s creative, fun, and unique.

![](/images/trad-goth-eye-makeup/sjdfiopsdufhdsjfhdskjfds-771x1024.png)

### 4. Bat Wing Eyeliner

Take your classic winged liner to the next level with a bat wing design. Apply your usual black eyeliner, but extend it into a bat wing shape at the outer corner of your eye.

**Pair it with dark eyeshadow on your lids and lower lash line for that true gothic vibe. Add long, voluminous lashes to make your eyes pop.**

This look is perfect for adding a touch of gothic elegance to your everyday style or for special occasions where you want to stand out.

![](/images/trad-goth-eye-makeup/jkhgugyfttytyfty-771x1024.png)

### 5. White and Black Dual Eyeshadow

For a truly striking look, try the white and black dual eyeshadow. Cover one eyelid in black eyeshadow and the other in white.

**This creates a stark, eye-catching contrast that’s perfect for making a statement. Finish with dramatic winged eyeliner and dark lashes to complete the look.**

This makeup is all about balance and contrast, showcasing your bold and creative side. It’s a daring look that’s sure to get noticed.

### 6. Victorian Lace Design

Add a touch of elegance to your gothic makeup with a Victorian lace design. Start with black eyeshadow on your lids.

This look is perfect for formal gothic events or when you want to showcase your artistic skills. It’s delicate, detailed, and absolutely beautiful.
