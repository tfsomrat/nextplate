### Step 2: Prepare Your Hair

Clean and detangling hair is the starting point for achieving a slick ponytail that looks professional and polished.

Begin by washing your hair with a **clarifying shampoo** to **get rid of any oils and residue**. Once clean, **towel dry your hair** to keep it damp but not dripping wet.

![](/images/how-to-do-a-slick-back-ponytail/How-to-Do-a-Slick-Back-Ponytail-Ultimate-Guide-2024-2-683x1024.jpg)

**Image Credit: [Freepik](http://www.freepik.com)**

Carefully **detangle your hair using a wide-tooth comb** to get rid of any major tangles without causing breakage.

![](/images/how-to-do-a-slick-back-ponytail/How-to-Do-a-Slick-Back-Ponytail-Ultimate-Guide-2024-3-683x1024.jpg)

**Image Credit: [Nordstrom](http://www.nordstrom.com)**

Follow this by **using a brush to smooth out** any remaining knots. Properly detangling and smoothing your hair at this stage will make it easier to gather into a sleek ponytail and prevent bumps along the hair shaft.

### Step 3: Decide on the Part

Choosing the right part for your slick ponytail is crucial for achieving the desired look. There are three main options to consider: **a middle part, a side part, or no part at all**. The decision largely depends on your face shape and personal preference.

A **middle part can give your ponytail a sleek**, **symmetrical appearance,** ideal for **those with oval or heart-shaped faces**. It **balances facial features and creates a classic, polished look.**

![](/images/how-to-do-a-slick-back-ponytail/How-to-Do-a-Slick-Back-Ponytail-Ultimate-Guide-2024-683x1024.jpg)

**Middle Part Ponytail by @[sophiabush](https://www.instagram.com/sophiabush/)**

On the other hand, a **side part can add volume and dimension**, making it a great **choice for round or square faces**. It introduces **asymmetry**, which can soften strong jawlines and add a hint of playfulness.

![](/images/how-to-do-a-slick-back-ponytail/How-to-Do-a-Slick-Back-Ponytail-Ultimate-Guide-2024-1-683x1024.jpg)

**Side Part Ponytail | Image Credit: [allthingshair.com](http://www.allthingshair.com)**

Finally, opting for **no part results in a smooth, streamlined finish that pulls all hair back** evenly. This style works well for those **looking to accentuate their bone structure** and highlight facial details.

### Step 4: Apply Styling Products

First, take a small amount of gel or pomade – about the size of a dime. Rub it between your palms to warm it up, then apply it evenly from the roots to the mid-lengths of your hair.

![](/images/how-to-do-a-slick-back-ponytail/How-to-Do-a-Slick-Back-Ponytail-Ultimate-Guide-2024-4-683x1024.jpg)

**Image Credit: [IStock](https://www.istockphoto.com/)**

Make sure you **distribute the product well by running your fingers or a fine-tooth comb** through your hair. This will help achieve that sleek, polished look by taming any stray hair and adding a healthy shine.

Finally, spray a **light mist of hairspray over your entire head and ponytail.** This locks in the sleek effect and ensures your hairstyle stays put all day without feeling stiff or greasy.

### Step 5: Brush Hair into a Ponytail

Using a **boar bristle brush, comb hai**r from the hairline back to the **desired ponytail position** (**high, mid, or low**).

The boar bristle brush is important here – it helps **distribute natural oils from your scalp down** the hair shaft, creating a natural sheen and smoother texture.

![](/images/how-to-do-a-slick-back-ponytail/How-to-Do-a-Slick-Back-Ponytail-Ultimate-Guide-2024-5-683x1024.jpg)

**Image Credit: [Sydnestyle](http://www.sydnestyle.com)**

For fine hair, **lightly mist with a volumizing spray before brushing** to add body and grip. If you have **thick hair, section it off and brush each portion** individually to ensure every strand is smooth.

Curly hair requires a slightly different approach; **apply a leave-in conditioner or styling cream before brushing to minimize frizz** and maintain curl definition.

Once gathered, secure the ponytail with an elastic band, preferably one that matches your hair color for a seamless look.

Use a small brush or toothbrush with a bit of hairspray or pomade to smooth down any stray hairs.
