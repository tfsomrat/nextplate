Short **afro hairstyles for 4C hair** are all about embracing the beauty and versatility of natural curls.

Styling short 4C hair can be both a fun and practical way to express your personal style, while maintaining healthy hair that thrives in its natural state.

![](/images/short-afro-hairstyles-4c-hair/short-afro-hairstyles-4c-hair-1-1024x576.jpg)

**In this article, we’ve gathered 25 short afro hairstyles for 4C hair that will inspire you to try something new.**

Each hairstyle is designed to accentuate the beauty of natural hair, providing options for every occasion, whether you’re going for a casual day look or something more sophisticated.

## Short Afro Hairstyles 4c Hair

![](/images/short-afro-hairstyles-4c-hair/short-afro-hairstyles-4c-hair-576x1024.jpg)

### 1. Twisted Front with Beads and Afro Bun

This style features **medium-sized twists** at the front of the head, adorned with **beads for extra flair**, while the rest of the hair is pulled into a **high puff bun**.

The twist details draw attention to the face, while the afro bun adds height and elegance. To achieve this look, create **sectioned parts at the front**, twist each section, and thread **decorative beads** onto the twists.

Gather the remaining hair into a **high puff bun**, using a wide-tooth comb or fingers to maintain the texture. This hairstyle is great for those with **4C hair** as it embraces the natural curl pattern while adding a playful yet stylish element.

### 2. Bantu Knots with Beaded Accent

Bantu knots are an **iconic protective style** for 4C hair, offering versatility and easy maintenance. This variation includes **beaded accents** on a front strand for an extra pop of personality.

To create the look, **part your hair into small sections**, then twist each section into a tight knot, securing it at the base. For the accent twist at the front, leave one section to braid or twist, and finish it off with **decorative beads**.

Bantu knots are perfect for short **4C hair**, allowing for easy styling while promoting **hair growth and protection**.

![](/images/short-afro-hairstyles-4c-hair/image-352.jpeg)

@[chinelo\_naturalhair](https://www.instagram.com/chinelo_naturalhair/)

### 3. Cornrows with Afro Puff

This hairstyle combines **tight cornrows** leading into a full, voluminous **afro puff** at the back. The **neatly parted cornrows** create structure while the afro puff adds volume.

To achieve this look, **cornrow the front section** of the hair straight back, focusing on keeping the parts clean and even. At the crown, secure the ends of the cornrows and fluff out the remaining hair into a **rounded afro puff**.

This style is great for **short 4C hair** as it’s protective and versatile, allowing the wearer to showcase both structured and natural elements.

![](/images/short-afro-hairstyles-4c-hair/image-353.jpeg)

### 4. Low Puff with Flat Twists

A chic and easy option, this style features **flat twists** along the side leading to a **low puff**. It’s perfect for someone who wants a neat and sleek look without compromising on texture.

To create it, **section off the sides** of the hair and create flat twists, starting from the hairline and working towards the back. Once the twists are in place, gather the remaining hair into a **low puff**.

This style works well for **short 4C hair** as it keeps the hair secure while highlighting the natural curls at the back. The flat twists also give it a sleek finish.

### 5. Side-Swept Afro Puff with Twists

This stylish look features a **side-swept section of twists** that flows naturally into a high **afro puff**. The twisted section at the front creates a defined contrast to the voluminous puff at the back.

To achieve this look, **section off the front of your hair**, then create **medium-sized twists**. Afterward, gather the rest of your hair into a high puff, securing it with a hair tie.

This combination highlights the natural texture of **4C hair** while adding structure with the twisted detail. It’s a versatile style that works for **short afro lengths**, and the puff adds a bold, voluminous touch to the look.

![](/images/short-afro-hairstyles-4c-hair/image-355.jpeg)

### 6. Cornrowed Twists with Defined Part

This hairstyle starts with **clean, defined cornrows** on the scalp, leading to beautifully twisted ends. It adds a sleek, polished look to the front with a touch of texture from the twists.

To achieve this style, **section your hair into rows**, create **cornrows** from the scalp, and twist the ends once the braid reaches midway. **Moisturize** the hair before twisting to ensure definition and reduce frizz.

This look is perfect for **short 4C hair**, as the twists maintain a natural volume while the cornrows keep the hair manageable and neat.

![](/images/short-afro-hairstyles-4c-hair/image-356.jpeg)

### 7. Wash-and-Go Natural Afro

A classic **wash-and-go** style for **4C hair**, this look showcases the natural beauty of tight, coily curls. The simplicity of this style makes it a go-to for those who want a low-maintenance yet stunning afro.

Start by washing and conditioning the hair, then apply a **leave-in conditioner and curl cream** to enhance curl definition. Let the hair air dry or use a diffuser to speed up the process while maintaining the shape.

The result is a soft, fluffy afro with beautifully defined curls, perfect for everyday wear and easy to achieve with minimal styling.

![](/images/short-afro-hairstyles-4c-hair/image-357.jpeg)

### 8. Cornrowed Front with Voluminous Afro Puff

This look combines **sleek cornrows** at the front with a textured, voluminous **afro puff** at the back. The contrast between the neatly braided front and the free-flowing puff adds dimension to the style.

To achieve this look, **cornrow the front section** of your hair towards the crown, then fluff out the rest of the hair into a puff. Perfect for both casual and semi-formal settings, this hairstyle is a showstopper.

This style works well for **short 4C hair**, offering a protective element with the cornrows while still embracing the fullness and texture of the afro puff.

### 9. Classic Afro with Volume

This classic **rounded afro** is a timeless way to showcase the natural texture of **4C hair**. The key to achieving this look is ensuring your hair is moisturized and well-defined.

Start by applying a **leave-in conditioner** or curl cream to freshly washed hair. Let your hair air dry or use a hooded dryer to lock in the curl pattern. Once dried, gently **pick the hair at the roots** to create volume, focusing on shaping the afro into a round form.

This style embraces the full, fluffy texture of natural hair while keeping it simple yet bold. It’s an ideal choice for short afros, enhancing the beauty of your natural coils.

### 10. Twin Puffs

This playful style features **two puffs** on either side of the head, adding a youthful and dynamic flair to short 4C hair.

To achieve this look, start by parting the hair down the middle, creating two even sections. **Moisturize and detangle** each section before gathering the hair into two high puffs, securing each with a hair tie.

You can use a wide-tooth comb to fluff the puffs for more volume. This hairstyle is perfect for a casual day out, as it’s quick to create while still looking chic and fun.

### 11. High Afro Puff

A **high afro puff** is a simple yet stunning way to style short **4C hair**. Start by brushing the hair upwards, gathering it into a **high ponytail** at the crown of your head.

Use a soft bristle brush to smooth the edges, and secure the puff with a hair tie. Once secured, **fluff the puff** to maximize volume, giving it a rounded shape.

This style works well for both casual and formal occasions, making it versatile and easy to achieve. The high puff is ideal for those with short 4C hair looking to add height and volume to their look.
