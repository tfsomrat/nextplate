When you’re gearing up for a lacrosse game—or simply looking for a sporty, stylish vibe—your hairstyle can make a big difference. For curly-haired players, this means finding that perfect blend of function and flair. Whether you’re sprinting down the field or cheering from the sidelines, the right hairstyle can boost confidence, keep hair out of your face, and even complement your game-day outfit. From secure buns to playful braids, there are countless ways to style curly hair that won’t leave you frustrated during an active day. In this list, I’ve curated 15 easy curly lacrosse hairstyles that are as practical as they are pretty. And if you’re on the hunt for a gift for your favorite curly-haired athlete, some of these styles come with Amazon product suggestions that make perfect game-day surprises.

## 1. High Puff with Headband

![](/images/lacrosse-hairstyles/evehogban_1738687964_3560695182745840560_2190042950-823x1024.webp)

**source @**[evehogban](https://www.instagram.com/evehogban/?api=1&hl=zh-cn&e=5c17cd45-938b-4bc2-83d1-4d4947a4d26c&g=5)

A high puff is a go-to style that combines ease with elegance. It’s perfect for naturally curly textures, especially when time is short. By simply brushing your curls up and securing them with a stretchy headband, you create a voluminous look that stays out of your face. This style is breathable and bouncy—ideal for intense practices and matches. For extra hold, a strong edge control gel helps tame flyaways and frame your face. Pair it with a stylish lacrosse-themed headband for the perfect sporty gift.

## 2. Braided Crown with Curls

![](/images/lacrosse-hairstyles/nikkimarie_thestylist_1724103086_3438348359393923088_463654439-976x1024.webp)

**source @**[nikkimarie\_thestylist](https://www.instagram.com/nikkimarie_thestylist/?e=beec2927-e6e9-4836-a17c-87bd96834e3c&g=5)

If you want something that holds well under a helmet or cap, the braided crown with curls is a beautiful option. This style keeps the front section of your hair tight and secure while letting your curls cascade naturally at the back. It works well for medium to long hair and adds a touch of flair that stands out on and off the field. To make it last through games, use a strong-hold gel and bobby pins. Gifting someone a premium curl-defining cream or braiding kit can elevate their entire styling routine.

## 3. Double Dutch Braids with Loose Ends

![](/images/lacrosse-hairstyles/jehat_1630274624_2651258168044682698_3613587-1024x1024.webp)

**source @**[jehat](https://www.instagram.com/jehat/?e=a48f85c1-ccc1-4bae-9e99-632fcf42fb66&g=5)

Double Dutch braids are a lacrosse favorite for a reason—they stay in place no matter how rough the game gets. For curly hair, the magic happens when you leave the ends loose, letting the curls shine. This combo gives structure at the top and movement at the bottom. It’s also helmet-friendly and low-maintenance throughout the day. Consider gifting a high-quality detangling brush or braid spray from Amazon to anyone who rocks this look regularly.

## 4. Messy Bun with Defined Edges

![](/images/lacrosse-hairstyles/kayy.hairstylist_1738884666_3562345234292245986_10691234869-819x1024.webp)

Clean edges bring polish to the style, and a few well-placed pins can hold everything in place. It’s perfect for practice days when you want to look cute with minimal effort. Edge control kits or curly hair ties make a thoughtful and practical gift for athletes who love this look.

## 5. Pineapple Ponytail
