The 1940s was an iconic decade for fashion, with hairstyles that continue to inspire vintage enthusiasts and modern stylists alike.

**As World War II shaped the world, women embraced practical yet glamorous hairstyles that balanced elegance with functionality.**

![](/images/1940s-hairstyles/1940s-Hairstyles-1-1024x614.jpg)

From sleek victory rolls to structured Hollywood curls, 1940s hairstyles were defined by their meticulous detail and signature looks, making them timeless classics.

**In this article, we explore 23 of the most memorable and iconic hairstyles from the 1940s.**

From victory rolls to finger waves, each style captures the spirit of the 1940s in its own unique way.

## 1940s Hairstyles

![](/images/1940s-hairstyles/1940s-Hairstyles-576x1024.jpg)

### 1. Victory Rolls Hairstyle

The **Victory Rolls** is one of the most iconic hairstyles of the 1940s. This look involves two voluminous rolls on the top of the head, framing the face. To achieve this, the hair is sectioned, backcombed, and rolled upward into thick curls, secured with pins.

This style reflects the **elegant yet bold** spirit of the wartime era, where women balanced **glamour with practicality**. The rest of the hair is left in soft waves cascading down the shoulders. Pairing this look with a red lip and vintage attire elevates its authenticity. **Use hot rollers** or curling irons to create the waves and backcombing for volume.

### 2. Poodle Hairstyle with Flowers

This **Poodle Hairstyle** features tightly curled bangs that resemble a fluffy “poodle” style, typical of the late 1940s. The top curls are arranged in a structured fashion, while the rest of the hair remains voluminous.

A floral accessory pinned near the crown gives this style a feminine touch. To recreate this, the hair is **tightly curled with rollers** or a curling iron and styled upwards into curls around the face. It’s ideal for those seeking a fun yet glamorous 1940s vintage vibe. Adding flowers is a classic way to enhance the charm of this look.

### 3. 1940s Beret Hairstyle

The **Beret Hairstyle** combines waves and sleek elegance. Hair is softly waved and pinned to one side, with the addition of a beret or hat, which was a typical 1940s accessory. The hat is placed slightly off-center for a stylish, casual flair.

The waves are created using hot rollers or curling irons, then brushed out for **smooth, structured waves**. This look is perfect for a day out or formal events. The use of accessories, such as gloves and berets, elevates the style and adds a touch of French vintage allure.

![](/images/1940s-hairstyles/image-92.jpeg)

### 4. Classic 1940s Half-Up Hairstyle

This **half-up hairstyle** is a vintage favorite that blends structure with soft glamour. The top section of hair is pulled back into subtle twists or rolls, while the remaining hair is styled into waves. The hair is **pinned at the back** with subtle volume, giving the look a balanced, polished appearance.

Often paired with bold accessories like **cat-eye sunglasses and gloves**, this look was popular in both casual and formal settings. The structured top section contrasts with the flowing waves, offering a balance of sophistication and playfulness.

### 5. Pompadour Victory Rolls

This **Pompadour Victory Rolls** style features a voluminous pompadour at the crown, paired with sleek waves. The front section is teased and backcombed to create height, then rolled into a bold pompadour.

The sides are slicked back, allowing the back of the hair to flow into soft, controlled waves. To achieve this look, **use a strong-hold hairspray** to keep the volume intact while the waves fall naturally. This style is perfect for formal events and brings a touch of **bold vintage glamour** to any look.

### 6. Curled Chignon with Decorative Hat

The **Curled Chignon** is a sophisticated updo, where tightly coiled curls form a low chignon at the nape of the neck. This style often includes a **decorative hat or fascinator**, typical of 1940s formal wear.

To recreate this style, set the hair in tight curls using **curling irons or hot rollers**, then pin the curls back to form the chignon. The hat adds an elegant touch, making this hairstyle ideal for vintage-themed parties or formal events. This look combines structured curls with a refined silhouette.

### 7. Sleek Rolls and Curls

This **Sleek Rolls and Curls** hairstyle is a quintessential 1940s look that blends sleek, rolled hair with tight curls at the back. The front sections are rolled smoothly to create volume, while the back is filled with structured curls.

This hairstyle is ideal for those who prefer a neat, vintage look with a touch of softness from the curls. To achieve this, **set the curls with rollers** and use a comb to smooth and roll the front sections. Finish with a ribbon or bow for an authentic 1940s touch.

### 8. Scarf-Wrapped Updo

The **Scarf-Wrapped Updo** is a practical yet stylish 1940s hairstyle, where the hair is neatly tied back and wrapped with a **decorative scarf**. This look was popular during wartime for its ease and functionality, while still offering a polished finish.

The hair is either twisted into a bun or styled into curls, then a scarf is wrapped around the head, leaving the ends tucked underneath. This style works well for casual outings and can be dressed up with accessories like pins or brooches for a more refined appearance.

### 12. Classic Finger Waves

The **Classic Finger Waves** hairstyle is one of the most iconic and structured looks of the 1940s. Hair is styled into smooth, defined S-shaped waves, often framing the face in a sophisticated, polished manner.

To recreate this style, wet the hair and use a fine-tooth comb to mold the waves, securing them in place with clips while the hair dries. This look reflects the **artistry of hairstyling** during the era and is perfect for formal occasions, adding a timeless and elegant flair.
