Have you ever admired the mysterious allure of goth makeup but felt it was too bold for everyday wear?

**Maybe you’re curious about how to incorporate a bit of that dark elegance into your daily routine without going all out.**

![](/images/subtle-goth-makeup/Subtle-Goth-Makeup-Looks-1024x576.jpg)

_Are you looking for ways to express your inner goth subtly, so it’s perfect for both day and night? Well, you’re in luck!_

**I’ve put together a list of 17 subtle goth makeup ideas that strike the perfect balance between edgy and understated.**

Whether you’re a makeup novice or a seasoned pro, these ideas will help you add a touch of goth charm to your look in a way that’s wearable.

**Let’s dive in!**

## 17 Subtle Goth Makeup Ideas

![](/images/subtle-goth-makeup/subtle-goth-makeup-looks-to-copy-in-2025-683x1024.webp)

### 1. Soft Smokey Eye with Nude Lips

If you want to dip your toes into goth makeup without going all out, try a soft smokey eye paired with nude lips.

**Start by blending shades of grey and black eyeshadow on your eyelids, focusing on creating a seamless transition.**

_This technique will give your eyes a mysterious yet subtle look. To balance the intensity of your eyes, opt for a nude lip color that complements your skin tone._

Finish off with a touch of highlighter on the inner corners of your eyes to brighten them up. This look is perfect for both day and night.

![](/images/subtle-goth-makeup/image-17.jpeg)

**Facebook/GlammedbyElena**

### 2. Dark Brown Eyeshadow with Soft Pink Lips

For a more understated goth vibe, you can go with dark brown eyeshadow and soft pink lips. Dark brown eyeshadow adds depth and drama without being too overpowering.

**Apply it to your lids and blend well to avoid any harsh lines. Then, swipe on a soft pink lip color to create a gentle contrast that’s still in line with the goth aesthetic.**

This combination is ideal if you’re looking to maintain a subtle, sophisticated look that’s perfect for any occasion.

![](/images/subtle-goth-makeup/dsjoifjeoiwjrweoirwer-771x1024.png)

### 3. Winged Eyeliner with Burgundy Lips

One of my favorite subtle goth looks is a classic winged eyeliner paired with burgundy lips. Start by drawing a sleek, precise wing on your upper lash line using a deep black liquid liner.

**The winged liner adds a touch of drama without being too bold. Then, apply a rich burgundy lipstick to complete the look.**

The deep red hue of the lips brings just the right amount of goth flair, while still keeping it subtle and wearable for every day.

![](/images/subtle-goth-makeup/image-18.jpeg)

**Facebook/Sara Jiri Permanent Make-Up Artist**

### 4. Plum Eyeshadow with Glossy Lips

You can achieve a subtle goth look by using plum eyeshadow and glossy lips. Apply plum eyeshadow on your eyelids and blend it into the crease for a soft, smoky effect.

**The plum shade adds a hint of color that’s perfect for a goth-inspired look without being too intense.**

To keep the overall look soft and balanced, finish with a glossy lip in a light shade. The gloss adds a touch of shine, making the look more modern and fresh.

![](/images/subtle-goth-makeup/image-19.jpeg)

**Facebook/Pretty Fine**

### 5. Subtle Cat Eyes with Berry Lips

Creating a subtle cat eye with berry lips is a great way to channel your inner goth. Start by drawing thin, precise cat eyes using black eyeliner.

**This classic look adds a touch of elegance and drama. Pair it with berry-colored lips for a hint of color that complements the goth aesthetic.**

The berry lips provide a pop of color without overwhelming the look. Add a light touch of blush to your cheeks to bring some warmth to your face, and you’re ready to go.

![](/images/subtle-goth-makeup/image-20.jpeg)

**Facebook/GLAM BAR** 

### 6. Grey Eyeshadow with Mauve Lips

For a soft and wearable goth look, try grey eyeshadow with mauve lips. Apply grey eyeshadow to your lids and blend it outwards for a smoky effect.

**The grey shade is perfect for creating a mysterious vibe without being too dark. Pair this with mauve lips to add a touch of color that’s still subtle and sophisticated.**

This combination is ideal for a day-to-night transition, making it perfect for any occasion.

### 7. Soft Black Liner with Natural Lips

You can never go wrong with soft black liner and natural lips for a subtle goth makeup look.

Pair it with a natural lip color to keep the focus on your eyes. This look is perfect for everyday wear, giving you a hint of goth without being too dramatic.
